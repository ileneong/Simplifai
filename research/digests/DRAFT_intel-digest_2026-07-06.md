## Top 3 this week

### 1. QuickBooks now has four AI agents running the back end of your business (July 1)
- **What changed:** Intuit began rolling out a suite of four AI agents inside QuickBooks Online from 1 July 2026. The Accounting Agent automatically categorises income and expense transactions and flags anomalies for your review. The Payments Agent predicts which invoices are likely to be paid late and sends customised reminders automatically. The Customer Agent organises leads and drafts outreach emails. The Finance Agent tracks cash flow trends, compares actuals to budget, and alerts you to problems proactively.
- **Source + date:** Intuit, investor relations press release and QuickBooks help centre (investors.intuit.com and quickbooks.intuit.com), 1 July 2026.
- **So what for the owner:** If QuickBooks Online is your accounting tool, open it today and check whether the new widget-based home screen is live on your account. If the agents are there, let the Accounting Agent run on one week's worth of uncategorised transactions and review what it chose, rather than going hands-off straight away. Intuit claims the Payments Agent gets businesses paid an average of 5 days faster, by sending reminders you would otherwise forget to send.
- **Honest caveat:** The rollout is staged across the US and not every account has it yet. Not available on QuickBooks Self-Employed or QuickBooks Desktop. Works best when your existing data is reasonably clean; garbage-in, garbage-out still applies. *(Regulated-claim flag: finance. Frame any resulting post as a time and admin win, not as financial or tax advice.)*
- **Content angle:** Single or Reel · Proof/news · hook: "QuickBooks just gave your accounts a team of four. Here is what they actually do."

### 2. Gemini now connects to your Google Business Profile (rolling out globally, July 2026)
- **What changed:** Google's Gemini app now lets you connect your Google Business Profile with a single tap. Once connected, Gemini can read your customer reviews, analyse what people are asking, and give you advice based on your actual listing data. You can ask it to draft a response to a review, update your opening hours, or post a business update, all without leaving the Gemini app. Google also introduced Business Notebooks inside Gemini: a dedicated space to keep business chats, notes, and data separate from personal conversations.
- **Source + date:** Google, Google Blog (blog.google/innovation-and-ai/products/gemini/gemini-features-for-businesses/) and 9to5Google (9to5google.com), announced 10 June 2026, global rollout continuing through July 2026.
- **So what for the owner:** For any clinic, studio, or practice with a Google listing, this collapses a multi-step chore (log into Google, find the review, think of a reply, post it) into a single Gemini conversation. Ask "Summarise my recent reviews and tell me what clients mention most often" and you get a pattern analysis in seconds. Then ask Gemini to draft a reply to your most recent review and post it. No tab-switching.
- **Honest caveat:** Not yet available in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom. The full feature set (including Business Notebooks) is on Google AI Pro at $19.99/month. The basic Google Business Profile connection is free on the Gemini app. Rolling out gradually, so it may not be on your account yet. *(Regulated-claim flag: wellness owners responding to health or treatment reviews must not make absolute outcome claims. Stick to service, experience, and care language in any AI-drafted review response.)*
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching · hook: "Your Google reviews now have an AI assistant. Here is what it can do."

### 3. You can now book client appointments through Claude or ChatGPT (July 2)
- **What changed:** Calendly's Model Context Protocol (MCP) integration reached general availability on 2 July 2026. You connect your Calendly account to Claude or ChatGPT once, and from then on you can manage your scheduling by typing in plain English inside the AI chat: find open slots, create single-use booking links, book a meeting, reschedule, or cancel, all without switching to the Calendly tab.
- **Source + date:** Calendly, Calendly Help Centre (calendly.com/help/connect-calendly-to-your-ai-tools), 2 July 2026.
- **So what for the owner:** For appointment-based businesses, client sessions, discovery calls, and consultations, the booking admin that currently lives across multiple browser tabs can now happen inside the same Claude or ChatGPT window where you are already writing emails or planning your week. Type "create a 45-minute booking link for new clients next Tuesday afternoon" and it is done.
- **Honest caveat:** Requires a paid Calendly plan. First-time MCP setup involves following a step-by-step guide (it is not a single click), so allow 10 minutes for the initial connection. This was in the watch list last week as "parked until it is genuinely one-click"; it is now GA but the setup step is still real. Callie, Calendly's in-email AI scheduling assistant, remains in private beta by invite.
- **Content angle:** Carousel or Reel · System teaching · hook: "Your AI assistant can book your client appointments now. Here is the set-up."

## Watch list

Setup is described as under 10 minutes via Meta Business Suite. Launched globally 3 June 2026, so just outside this week's window, but still in active rollout and setup phase for many owners. Source: TechCrunch, 3 June 2026. For wellness owners receiving high volumes of "how much is a session" messages: this is worth prioritising.

Complex multi-step requests, longer documents, and chained tasks handle noticeably better. No action needed; if you use Claude.ai, you are already on it. Source: Anthropic (anthropic.com/news), 30 June 2026.

- **Canva Grow 2.0: design, publish, and track ads from one screen.** Launched 25 June 2026 at Cannes Lions. You build the ad in Canva, publish it directly to Meta, LinkedIn, or TikTok from the same screen, and see real-time performance data come back without leaving Canva. AI Ad Tagging labels which creative elements are driving results; an Automatic Refresh generator suggests new ad concepts based on what is working. Paid Canva for Teams tier; exact Grow 2.0 pricing check canva.com/pricing. Source: BusinessWire and Canva Newsroom (businesswire.com and canva.com/newsroom), 25 June 2026.

- **Instagram DM scheduling and translation for business accounts.** Business accounts can now schedule direct messages up to 29 days in advance and translate incoming DMs from 99 languages with one tap. Rolling out to business accounts, June 2026 (exact date within this window not confirmed by a single primary source with a day-level date). Source: Social Media Today (socialmediatoday.com) and NapoleonCat (napoleoncat.com), June 2026. *Unconfirmed exact date.*

- **HubSpot Brand Knowledge now available to Starter and above.** Store your business tone, positioning, and key information in one settings section; all Breeze AI-generated content across HubSpot draws from it automatically. If you use HubSpot CRM, spend 10 minutes filling it in now so every AI-drafted email is in your voice from day one. Source: HubSpot Community (community.hubspot.com), June 2026.

- **Notion 3.6 adds speaker identification to AI Meeting Notes.** From 1 July 2026, Notion's meeting notes now attribute comments to the person who said them and auto-assign follow-up tasks accordingly. You can also upload an audio recording (not just live meetings) and Notion will transcribe and summarise it. Requires the Notion AI add-on. Source: Notion (notion.com/releases), 1 July 2026.

- **Adobe Express adds audio extraction from video (July 2).** You can now pull the audio track out of any video clip inside Adobe Express and edit it as a separate element. - **ChatGPT personal finance dashboard expands to Plus tier (US only).** Previously Pro-only, the ability to connect bank and credit card accounts (via Plaid) and ask questions about your actual spending is now available to ChatGPT Plus subscribers in the US. If you have existing Zaps with AI steps, log in and check your task usage and consider switching individual steps to Standard tier for simple text tasks. Source: Zapier Help Centre (help.zapier.com), 15 June 2026. *This is a cost watch-out, not a feature gain.*

# Simplifai Research — How businesses can easily build workflows with AI today

*Topic deep-dive (not the weekly tool sweep). Researched 2026-06-27 with AI assistance; every claim traced to a primary or named source. Scope: what a non-marketer small-business owner can actually set up this week.*

## The one thing to understand first

"Building a workflow with AI" splits into two layers, and owners confuse them. Lead with this distinction in any post.

- **Layer 1 — the reusable AI assistant (no automation, no code, free today).** You set up an AI helper *once* with standing instructions plus your own reference material, then reuse it forever instead of re-explaining yourself every time. This is the genuinely easy, free, this-week answer for most owners. Tools: ChatGPT Custom GPTs, Google Gemini Gems, Claude Projects.
- **Layer 2 — the connected automation (it runs without you).** This links your actual apps (Gmail, Sheets, your scheduler, your books) and runs the job, so work happens while you sleep. The big shift in 2026 is that you now describe it in plain English and the tool builds a draft. Two options are genuinely owner-ready today: **Zapier Copilot** (free, simplest on-ramp) and **Claude for Small Business** (paid plan, but purpose-built for owners with a human-approval gate). Make Maia and Microsoft Copilot Studio are *not yet owner-ready* and belong in the "what's coming" bucket.

**The "today" filter (the answer to the actual question):** if an owner asked "what can I set up this week," the honest answers are: a reusable AI assistant (Layer 1, free), Zapier Copilot's free two-step Zaps (Layer 2, free), and Claude for Small Business (Layer 2, needs a paid Claude plan but is built for exactly this). Everything else is trial-gated or not generally available yet. Make Maia is early access (no public pricing, can't sign up today). Copilot Studio is early-release, business-tier, no solo free path. Do not present those two as "set it up today" options.

Owner truth: most owners need Layer 1, think they need Layer 2, and stall because Layer 2 sounds hard. The honest, useful message is "start at Layer 1, it is free and takes 15 minutes."

## Layer 1 — Reusable AI assistants (the easy, free entry point)

### A. ChatGPT Custom GPTs / Gemini Gems / Claude Projects
- **What changed / what it is:** All three major assistants now let you save a custom helper with standing instructions and your own uploaded knowledge (your brand voice, your services, your FAQs), with no code and no developer. You build it once and reuse it. Gemini's version is "Gems," ChatGPT's is "Custom GPTs," Claude's is "Projects."
- **Source + date:** Per 2026 comparison roundups (StackViv "Custom GPTs, Gems & Claude Projects: Full 2026 Guide"; Builts AI; Zapier's own Claude-vs-ChatGPT guide, 2026). *These are secondary; before this becomes a published claim, confirm current free-tier access on each tool's own help page, since tiers shift.*
- **So what for the owner:** Stop re-explaining your business to the AI every session. Set your brand voice and the facts about your business once, and every caption, reply, or email draft comes out already sounding like you. Setup is roughly 15 minutes and is available without a subscription on entry tiers.
- **Honest caveat:** This is an assistant, not automation. It does not send anything, watch your inbox, or run on its own; you still open it and ask. Notion AI sits in this same layer and notably cannot reach external tools either. Pick by where your work already lives: Claude Projects for document and writing-heavy work, Custom GPTs when you want web browsing or image generation built in, Gems if you are already in Google.
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching · hook: "Set up your AI once, stop re-explaining your business every time."

## Layer 2 — Connected automation you can actually set up today

Only one option here clears the "today, free or near-free, non-technical" bar.

### B. Zapier Copilot — describe a workflow, it builds a draft Zap
- **What changed:** Zapier Copilot lets you describe an automation in plain language and watch it assemble a rough working version across Zapier's app connections, no formula-building by hand. Copilot launched September 2025 and is now woven across the platform; Zapier also has autonomous "Agents" for goal-based tasks.
- **Source + date:** Zapier, "What is Zapier Copilot?" and "Use the power of AI to generate Zaps" help pages (help.zapier.com); Copilot natural-language builder dated September 2025; Agents reached general availability May 2025.
- **So what for the owner:** The most accessible Layer-2 on-ramp. The free plan includes Copilot and lets you run simple two-step automations (one trigger, one action), so an owner can genuinely build a "new form submission goes to a spreadsheet" flow by typing, for free.
- **Honest caveat:** The free plan caps you at 100 tasks a month and two-step Zaps; multi-step automations and the autonomous Agents need a paid plan (Agents open only a 7-day trial first). Copilot builds a *rough* version you still review and fix, it is a first draft, not a finished system.
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching · hook: "Build your first automation by typing one sentence."

### B2. Claude for Small Business — owner-built workflows with an approval gate
- **What changed:** Anthropic shipped a packaged product aimed squarely at small business owners: 15 ready-to-run agentic workflows and 15 reusable "skills" across finance, operations, sales, marketing, HR, and customer service, plus connectors into the tools owners already use. You toggle it on inside Claude Cowork, connect your apps, and pick the job; Claude does the work and you approve before anything sends, posts, or pays. They also released a free "AI fluency" training course (a 4D framework) co-developed with PayPal.
- **Source + date:** Anthropic, "Introducing Claude for Small Business" (anthropic.com/news), 13 May 2026.
- **So what for the owner:** This is the most owner-shaped answer to the whole question. Unlike a blank automation builder, the workflows are pre-built for real small-business jobs (monthly close, cash forecast, lead triage, campaign analysis and asset generation), and the connectors include marketing-relevant tools like Canva, HubSpot, and Google Workspace. The built-in "approve before it sends, posts, or pays" step is exactly the human-in-the-loop safeguard a careful owner wants.
- **Honest caveat:** It needs a paid Claude plan (the announcement does not price the package itself; the genuinely free part is the AI fluency course). Your existing app permissions still apply, so Claude can only touch what you already can. And like any agentic tool, the approval step matters precisely because it can act, do not switch it to run end-to-end on money or client messages until you trust it.
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching · hook: "Claude now ships ready-made workflows for small businesses, with an approval step before anything sends."
- **Note for Simplifai:** this is close to Simplifai's own pitch ("your AI marketing team, built in a weekend"). Treat it as a tool inside the Stack and a teach-the-owner topic, not a competitor to fear. The owner still needs the system, voice, and judgement Simplifai provides around it.

### Where Claude's three pieces fit (since owners ask "what is Claude for workflows?")
Claude shows up at both layers, which is why it confuses people:
- **Claude Projects** — Layer 1, the reusable assistant (see A). Free-tier accessible. Best for document and writing-heavy work.
- **Claude Skills** — saved repeatable task recipes you reuse, the building blocks inside the above.
- **Claude for Small Business / Cowork** — Layer 2, the connected, acting workflows (see B2). Paid.

So "use Claude for workflows" can mean a free reusable assistant *or* a paid acting automation. Always say which one you mean.

## Not yet for owners — coming, but not a "today" answer

These two are real and worth tracking, but an owner cannot quickly set either up today. They are here so the digest is honest about the gap between the marketing headline ("build by typing") and what a solo owner can actually use this week. **Do not feature either as a do-it-now option.**

### C. Make Maia — conversational scenario builder *(early access, not general release)*
- **What changed:** Make introduced Maia, a build-time AI assistant that turns a plain-language description into a configured automation inside Make's visual builder, through a back-and-forth conversation rather than one-shot generation. Make also shipped a separate autonomous "AI Agents" feature in February 2026.
- **Source + date:** Make, "What to know about building AI and automation with Maia by Make" (make.com/blog); AI Agents released February 2026. *Maia itself is not yet in general availability as of mid-2026; an early-access program is opening. Flag as not-yet-GA in any post.*
- **So what for the owner:** Promising for owners who want more power than Zapier's two free steps while still seeing the logic laid out visually for review. The visual transparency (you watch the scenario take shape and can tweak it) suits owners who do not fully trust a black box.
- **Honest caveat:** Not generally available yet, so per-plan access and price are not confirmed. Do not present Maia as something an owner can sign up for today; treat it as "coming, worth watching."
- **Content angle:** Watch-list item, or a single "what's coming" post once GA lands.

### D. Microsoft Copilot Studio — plain-English agents inside Microsoft 365 *(business-tier, early release)*
- **What changed:** Copilot Studio lets you describe an agent in plain language (its instructions, triggers, knowledge, and tools) and added a redesigned single-canvas "workflows" experience in 2026 that mixes fixed business logic with AI decision steps, plus agents that can operate app interfaces without an API.
- **Source + date:** Microsoft Copilot Blog, "What's new in Copilot Studio" April and May 2026 updates (microsoft.com). The new workflows experience and orchestration upgrades are in *early release environments*, not general availability; voice features are North America only.
- **So what for the owner:** Most relevant to owners already paying for Microsoft 365 who want AI agents acting inside the tools they already use, rather than adding another subscription.
- **Honest caveat:** This is the most business/enterprise-leaning option of the four and the least "set up in 15 minutes." No clear free tier for a solo owner, and key pieces are still early release. Not the starting point for a non-technical owner this week.
- **Content angle:** Watch-list item; only worth a post for the Microsoft-365 segment of the audience.

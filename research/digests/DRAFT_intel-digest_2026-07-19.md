## Top 3 this week

### 1. ChatGPT can now search everything you have ever put into it
- **What changed:** OpenAI rolled out a unified search across your past chats, projects, images and documents, on web, iOS and Android, with filters to narrow by content type. It is on every plan, free included.
- **Source + date:** OpenAI, ChatGPT release notes (help.openai.com), 14 July 2026. *The release-notes page blocked automated fetching this sweep; verify the exact entry there before publishing.*
- **So what for the owner:** All those drafts you wrote weeks ago, the caption that landed, the reply you polished for a tricky customer, the price list you pasted in, stop being lost in an endless scroll. Type a few words and the old work comes back. Your chat history quietly becomes a searchable filing cabinet for your business.
- **Honest caveat:** Search finds things, it does not organise them; projects are still the tidier habit. And it is a reminder that whatever you paste in is stored, so keep client names and personal details out of your chats in the first place.
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching · hook: "That caption you wrote in May? Your AI can find it now."

### 2. Google's Gemini now comes with small-business tools built in
- **What changed:** Google is rolling out Gemini features aimed squarely at small businesses: connect your Google Business Profile so Gemini answers with your actual brand and customer context, plus new Business notebooks to organise workflows, track tasks and generate content grounded in your business.
- **Source + date:** Google, The Keyword blog (blog.google, "Save time and grow your business with new Gemini tools"), rolling out globally July 2026. *Google's blog blocked automated fetching this sweep; verify the exact post and rollout wording before publishing.*
- **So what for the owner:** Instead of re-explaining your business to a blank chat window, you point Gemini at the profile you already keep for Google Maps and Search. Replies, descriptions and content come out sounding like your business, and the notebook keeps the week's jobs in one place.
- **Honest caveat:** It is a gradual rollout, so it may not be on your account yet, and it needs an up-to-date Google Business Profile to be useful. Think before connecting anything holding customer data; check what is shared in your Google settings first.
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching · hook: "Google already knows your business. Now its AI can use that for you."

### 3. Claude Cowork now runs on your phone and in the browser
- **What changed:** Anthropic's Claude Cowork, previously desktop-app only, is expanding to the web at claude.ai and to iOS and Android, in beta. Remote sessions run on Anthropic's servers, so a task you hand over keeps running with your laptop closed.
- **Source + date:** Anthropic, Claude blog (claude.com/blog/cowork-web-mobile), announced 7 July 2026.
- **So what for the owner:** This is the hand-off habit going mobile: set a task at your desk, then check the finished work from your phone between clients. Scheduled jobs, like a weekly report or content draft, no longer need your computer awake.
- **Honest caveat:** It is a beta, rolling out over several weeks, and it starts with the top-tier Max plan, with more plans to follow. If you are on a lower plan, this is one to watch, not one to use this week.
- **Content angle:** Reel or single · Proof / news · hook: "Set the task at your desk. Pick up the finished work from your phone."

## Top 3 this week

### 1. Adobe Express opened its conversational AI Assistant to the free plan, no waitlist
- **What changed:** Adobe Express moved its AI Assistant beta out of Premium-only and onto the free plan, with no waitlist. You describe what you want in plain words and it builds or edits the design for you, including replacing the text inside an image so it matches the original style, cleaning up backgrounds, and adding or deleting pages by prompt.
- **Source + date:** Adobe, Adobe Express "What's new" and AI Assistant help pages (helpx.adobe.com), documentation updated 18 June 2026; first introduced at Adobe MAX, October 2025, Premium-only until now.
- **So what for the owner:** A genuinely free way to make on-brand marketing graphics by typing, not by learning a design tool. If you have been priced out of a Canva paid plan or you just want a second free option, you can now describe a post and get an editable draft without a subscription.
- **Honest caveat:** It is still a beta, it is desktop web only for now, and the free tier gives you a small daily allotment of AI generations that resets each day, so it is fine for a few posts a week, not for batch-producing a month at once. Set your brand colours and fonts first or "on-brand" will not mean much.
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching · hook: "A free way to make on-brand graphics by typing. Here's the 5-minute version."

### 2. Google Sheets can now fix a broken formula in one click
- **What changed:** Gemini in Google Sheets gained a one-click formula fix. When a formula throws an error, it reads the surrounding data, explains in plain language what went wrong, and offers a corrected version you can accept.
- **Source + date:** Google, Google Workspace Updates blog (workspaceupdates.googleblog.com), announced 22 June 2026, rolling out gradually over the following two weeks.
- **So what for the owner:** If you track your content calendar, your numbers, or your simple reporting in a spreadsheet, you no longer lose twenty minutes to a cryptic #ERROR! message. It is included in paid Workspace plans you likely already have, so there is nothing new to buy.
- **Honest caveat:** It only helps inside Google Sheets, not Excel, and it is rolling out gradually so it may not be on your account yet. It fixes the formula, it does not check that your underlying numbers are right, that part is still on you.
- **Content angle:** Single or Carousel · System teaching · hook: "Stop losing 20 minutes to a spreadsheet error. One click now fixes it."

### 3. Instagram is rolling out an account-level "AI Creator" label more broadly
- **What changed:** Instagram is expanding its account-level "AI Creator" label, which appears on a creator's bio and alongside their posts and Reels in Feed and Explore, flagging accounts that regularly publish AI-generated or AI-assisted content. It began as a test in early May and is now widening.
- **Source + date:** Instagram via Social Media Today, "Instagram adds AI Creator labels" (socialmediatoday.com), test reported early May 2026, broader rollout noted in late-June 2026 update roundups (SocialBee, 23 June 2026). *Rollout pace unconfirmed, verify against Instagram's own newsroom before posting as fact.*
- **So what for the owner:** This is the case for disclosing your AI use rather than hiding it. Owners who are open about using AI, and who still sound like themselves, keep trust. The label only stings if you were pretending. It also signals where the platform is heading, so build the habit now.
- **Content angle:** Reel or single · Honest limits / reframe · hook: "Instagram is labelling AI content. Here's why that's good news if you do it right."

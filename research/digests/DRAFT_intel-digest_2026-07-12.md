## Top 3 this week

### 1. Claude Cowork now goes where you go
- **What changed:** Anthropic expanded Claude Cowork from desktop to web and mobile. Sessions and files now live in your Claude account rather than on one computer, work keeps running after you close the laptop, and scheduled tasks run even when no device is online. Chat and Cowork now share one home for projects and files.
- **Source + date:** Anthropic announcement, 7 July 2026 (anthropic.com news; covered same day by TechCrunch and 9to5Mac). *Verify the exact plan availability line at the primary announcement before publishing.*
- **So what for the owner:** The AI work desk stops being a place you sit and becomes something you carry. Start a job at the desk in the morning, check and approve it from your phone between clients. Set the weekly jobs (enquiry triage, invoice chase, content drafts) as scheduled tasks and they run without you babysitting a laptop.
- **Honest caveat:** It is rolling out over several weeks and starts with the top-tier Max plan, with other plans to follow, so it may not be on your account yet. Remote sessions are in beta. The usual rule stands: never put client names, health details, or sensitive business data into any session.
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching · hook: "Your AI desk now goes where you go."

### 2. Xero's assistant learned to chase your invoices for you
- **What changed:** At Xerocon London, Xero announced a new set of AI tools inside JAX, its plain-English finance assistant: smart document capture (JAX reads a source document and extracts the data into Xero), auto bank reconciliation that matches transactions in real time, and payment follow-ups where JAX reads each customer's payment history and runs a tailored, adapting collection plan. It can also create and send quotes and invoices over email, SMS, and WhatsApp.
- **Source + date:** Xero, Xerocon London announcements, 8 July 2026 (xero.com media release; covered by CPA Practice Advisor and TechRepublic).
- **So what for the owner:** Chasing late payments is the admin job owners hate most and skip most. An assistant that follows up politely, persistently, and automatically is real money recovered and a real hour back each week. This builds on JAX being switched on for every Xero account since 1 June (last fortnight's digest).
- **Honest caveat:** These were announced on stage and are landing in stages; some pieces (like bank statement extraction) were described as coming rather than live, so check what is actually in your account before relying on it. And as always: treat JAX as a fast admin assistant, not financial or tax advice, and eyeball everything before it sends. *(Regulated-claim flag: finance. Frame any post as a time and admin win, never as financial advice.)*
- **Content angle:** Carousel or single · System teaching · hook: "Your accounting tool can now chase late invoices for you. Politely."

### 3. OpenAI launched ChatGPT Work, an AI that hands you finished documents
- **What changed:** OpenAI released ChatGPT Work, a new agent mode powered by GPT-5.6 that takes a goal, works for as long as it needs, and returns finished documents, spreadsheets, presentations, reports, and simple websites (Sites, in public beta). It ships inside a new desktop app for Mac and Windows that combines Chat, Work, and Codex.
- **Source + date:** OpenAI announcement, 9 July 2026 (openai.com; covered same day by Forbes and Bloomberg). *openai.com was unreachable from this research environment; confirm the free-tier line at the primary page before publishing.*
- **So what for the owner:** The pitch is real: "turn my notes into a client proposal" or "build me a price list spreadsheet" and get a finished file back, not a draft in a chat window. For owners already paying for ChatGPT, this is worth a try on one bounded admin job.
- **Honest caveat:** It launched on paid plans first (Pro, Enterprise, and Edu, with Plus and Business following days later); reports on free-plan access conflict, so assume paid-only for now. And "finished" still means finished-looking: you remain the editor and the approver, especially on anything with numbers or claims in it.
- **Content angle:** Reel or single · Honest limits · hook: "AI now hands you finished documents. Here is what to check before you trust one."

## Top 3 this week

### 1. Instagram's teleprompter is now inside the main app, not a separate download

- **What changed:** Instagram moved its teleprompter tool out of the standalone Edits app and into the main Instagram camera for Reels. Write or paste your script, set the scroll speed, and the words roll across your screen right next to the front lens while you record. No third-party app, no subscription, no extra step.
- **Source + date:** SocialBee, "Instagram Updates, News and Features" (socialbee.com/blog/instagram-updates), confirmed active June 23, 2026. Supporting coverage: Metricool blog, Social Media Today. Original feature traced to Instagram's own Edits app rollout.
- **So what for the owner:** A wellness coach who freezes on camera or loses their train of thought mid-clip can now record a clear, confident 30-second tutorial without memorising anything. Film a tip, a service intro, or a FAQ answer in the time it takes to write the script. Free and available in your existing app.
- **Honest caveat:** Gradual rollout, so check for your app update first. The teleprompter scrolls at a fixed speed initially so you may need a few takes to match your pace. Works on the front camera only. REGULATED CLAIM FLAG: if you are a health practitioner, do not use this to record claims about treatment outcomes or health benefits. Those need TGA/FDA-appropriate disclaimers and are outside the scope of a social post.
- **Content angle:** Carousel or single image · Proof / news pillar · hook: "Instagram just solved the 'I freeze on camera' problem."

### 2. Canva's two most practical new features from Canva Create 2026 are rolling out now

- **What changed:** Two additions from the Canva Create 2026 event are now in general rollout: (1) **Canva Sheets AI**, which lets you connect a spreadsheet to your design so content updates automatically when the data changes, and (2) **Offline Mode**, which lets you open, edit, and save designs without an internet connection, syncing when you reconnect.
- **Source + date:** Canva, "More from Canva Create: Canva Offline, Pro Design, Learn Grid and Print Shop" (canva.com/newsroom/news/canva-create-2026-launches), Canva Create 2026, rolling out late June 2026. Canva Create event dates confirmed via Canva newsroom.
- **So what for the owner:** Sheets AI means a price list, a class timetable, or a product catalogue can feed directly into a branded Canva template. Update the spreadsheet, the graphic updates itself. Offline Mode means you can draft content on a phone with patchy internet, which is most real-world conditions. Both reduce the weekly design workload without requiring any new skills.
- **Honest caveat:** Sheets AI works best with structured, table-style data. It will not interpret free-text notes or loosely formatted spreadsheets well. Offline Mode is available on paid tiers first. Rollout is gradual so not all accounts have access yet.
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching pillar · hook: "Your Canva designs can now update themselves when your prices change."

### 3. Meta launched a beta hub where AI writes your partnership and ad copy inside Facebook

- **What changed:** Meta launched a Creator Marketing Hub in beta on June 23, 2026, merging its Creator Marketplace and Partnership Ads tools. Inside the hub, AI generates ad copy and creative variations for collaborations and boosted posts, based on the brand and product details you enter.
- **Source + date:** AlmCorp, "Meta Rolls Out New AI Ad Tools on Facebook and Instagram" (almcorp.com/news), June 23, 2026. Hub is in beta; full rollout expected second half of 2026.
- **So what for the owner:** If you run occasional paid promotions or partner with another wellness business on a campaign, the AI writes the ad copy for you inside Meta's platform, removing the blank-page moment before a boosted post. The creative brief becomes a short form, not a writing exercise.
- **Honest caveat:** Beta access only. Not every account has the hub yet. A minimum follower count or ad-spend history may be required. This is for ads and paid collaborations, not organic posts. REGULATED CLAIM FLAG: any paid wellness content on Meta must comply with Meta's health advertising policies and TGA/FTC rules on therapeutic claims.
- **Content angle:** Watch list for now (beta) · Escalate to carousel when general availability is confirmed.

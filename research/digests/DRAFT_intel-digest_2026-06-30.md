## Top 3 this week

### 1. ChatGPT just got much better at typing for you
- **What changed:** OpenAI rolled out a new speech-to-text model for dictation across all ChatGPT plans, including the free plan. Transcription is more accurate across languages and accents, including when a speaker mixes languages mid-sentence. You tap the mic, talk, and it writes.
- **Source + date:** OpenAI, ChatGPT release notes (help.openai.com), June 2026. *Verify the exact line at the primary release-notes page before publishing.*
- **So what for the owner:** This is hands-free first drafts. Between clients, in the car park, walking the dog, you can talk out a caption, a client follow-up, or a week of post ideas and have it typed cleanly, no keyboard. For an owner who thinks faster than they type, this turns dead minutes into drafts. It is free.
- **Honest caveat:** It writes what you say, voice and all, so the first pass will ramble; you still edit for the Simplifai-style tightness. And never dictate a client's name, health details, or any personal information into it. Talk in general terms, fill the specifics in yourself.
- **Content angle:** Carousel · System teaching · hook: "Talk, do not type. Your next week of content, drafted between clients."

### 2. Google can now take the notes in your calls for you
- **What changed:** Gemini in Google Workspace gained "Take notes for me," which records and transcribes a call, summarises the key points, pulls out the action items, and sends the summary to your Gmail. It began rolling out 22 June 2026.
- **Source + date:** Google, Google Workspace Updates blog (workspaceupdates.googleblog.com), rollout began 22 June 2026, arriving gradually over the following weeks.
- **So what for the owner:** If you run discovery calls, supplier calls, or team check-ins, you stop choosing between listening and scribbling. You get a clean summary and a to-do list without lifting a finger, included in the Workspace plan you likely already pay for.
- **Honest caveat:** Recording people has rules. Get clear consent before you hit record, and for a wellness owner this matters doubly: do not record a client's health consultation or anything covering personal health information. Keep this for internal and business calls, not clinical sessions. It is also rolling out gradually, so it may not be on your account yet. *(Regulated-claim flag: health/privacy. Any resulting post must say "with consent" and steer owners away from recording clinical or client-health calls.)*
- **Content angle:** Single or Reel · Honest limits · hook: "AI can take your call notes now. Here is the one rule you cannot skip."

### 3. Xero's AI assistant is now switched on for every account
- **What changed:** Just Ask Xero (JAX), Xero's plain-English AI finance assistant, moved out of beta and onto Xero's standard terms as of 1 June 2026, so it is available across all Xero organisations by user role, no opt-in needed. You ask it in normal words to draft an invoice or a quote, or to answer a question about who has paid and who has not.
- **Source + date:** Xero, Xero Central "About Just Ask Xero (JAX)" and Xero media releases (central.xero.com, xero.com), beta-to-standard transition effective 1 June 2026.
- **So what for the owner:** Invoicing and "did that client ever pay me" are exactly the admin jobs owners put off. Asking in plain English, instead of clicking through menus, lowers the bar to staying on top of the money side each week.
- **Honest caveat:** Access to JAX via SMS, WhatsApp and email has been discontinued; it now lives inside Xero itself, so it is web and app only. Treat its answers as a fast draft and a prompt, not as financial or tax advice, and always eyeball the numbers before you send anything. *(Regulated-claim flag: finance. Frame any post as a time and admin win, never as tax or financial advice.)*
- **Content angle:** Single or Carousel · System teaching · hook: "Ask your accounting tool, in plain English, who still owes you money."

## Watch list

Worth a closer look next week, but note the catch for owners: the full conversational AI 2.0, Memory and Connectors are **paid-plan only**; the free plan gets a capped monthly allowance (around 200 standard AI uses or 20 premium uses). Not a "free upgrade" story. (Canva newsroom, canva.com, April 2026; help.canva.com on AI access.)
- **Notion AI opened single-database querying on the Business plan** (previously Enterprise-only), so owners can point the assistant at one database, like a client list or content calendar, and ask questions of just that. (Notion releases, notion.com, June 2026.)
- **Zapier — two heads-ups, not a how-to.** AI by Zapier moved to model-based pricing from 15 June 2026 (steps now cost more on the higher model tiers), and its AI Guardrails (auto-scanning for personal information, prompt injection and toxic content) reached general availability. The pricing change is a cost watch-out for owners already running AI Zaps. (Zapier blog + help.zapier.com, June 2026.)
- **Calendly MCP** lets you find slots, book, and update your availability by asking an AI chatbot. Promising for appointment-based wellness owners, but MCP setup is still a touch technical; parked until it is genuinely one-click. (Calendly, 2026.)
- **HubSpot Breeze Assistant** added a free mobile assistant (meeting prep, file uploads) and in-chat charts across all tiers including free CRM. The advanced Breeze *Agents* remain paid. (HubSpot, hubspot.com, 2026.)

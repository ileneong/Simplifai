## Caption

An AI can now answer your customer DMs and book appointments for you. That is one of 3 things that changed in AI this week, and all of them save you time if you run a small business. No hype, no jargon. Here they are.

1. Canva AI 2.0 is here. You describe what you want in plain words and it builds a near-finished, on-brand design you can then edit. The blank-canvas freeze that stops most owners? It takes the first swing for you. (Source: Canva newsroom, Canva Create 2026. Rolling out gradually, best features on paid tiers.)

2. ChatGPT rebuilt its memory this month, with roughly double the capacity and clearer controls to see and edit what it knows about you. It can finally hold your brand voice and your customer across chats, so you stop re-explaining your business every time. (Source: OpenAI release notes, early June 2026.)

3. Meta's AI agent can now answer customer questions and book appointments inside your Instagram DMs. Useful, with one honest caveat: let it be the first responder, not the voice of your brand. Set guardrails and hand off to a human. (Source: Meta via TechCrunch, June 2026.)

One rule under all of it: never paste a customer's personal details into any AI tool. Credible beats fast.

None of these is a magic trick. They are pieces you plug into a system you run in a few hours a week, with you still in control. That is the whole Simplifai idea.

01
The Versatile Drafter

ChatGPT

GPT-4o · Available at chat.openai.com

ChatGPT is the most widely used AI tool — and for good reason. It's forgiving with prompts, quick to iterate, and handles almost any writing task you throw at it. It won't always nail your brand voice on the first try, but ask it for three versions and you'll have a solid starting point in under a minute.

Example Prompt

"Write 3 versions of an Instagram caption for a small business coach launching a new 6-week group program. Warm, encouraging tone. Keep each under 150 words."

What to Expect

Three distinct drafts, each with a slightly different angle — one more inspirational, one more direct, one more community-focused. The tone is usually solid, though it may lean a little generic without more brand context. These are great starting points to edit from, not finished copy.

1

Always ask for multiple versionsChatGPT thrives on iteration. "Give me 3 options" or "write a short and a long version" gets you more useful output than a single draft.

2

Keep prompts simple to startYou don't need to over-engineer it. A clear, plain-language ask gets a strong first draft — then refine from there in the same chat window.

02
The Brand Voice Keeper

Claude

Anthropic · Available at claude.ai

Claude is where brand voice really shines. Give it context — who you are, what you sound like, what you don't want — and it will hold that through an entire piece of content. It's also excellent for longer-form writing: email sequences, blog posts, workshop descriptions. The more you brief it, the better it gets.

Example Prompt

"You are a content writer for a mindfulness coaching business. Our voice is warm, grounded, and evidence-informed — never hype, never clinical. Write an Instagram caption announcing a new 6-week mindfulness workshop. Include a gentle CTA. Under 150 words."

What to Expect

A noticeably more nuanced, on-voice result — especially if you've provided clear brand context. Claude naturally avoids hype language and defaulting to filler phrases. The output will feel considered rather than generated. Great for brands with a specific personality they need to protect.

1

Brief it like a new team memberStart with: "You are a content writer for [business name]. Our voice is [3 adjectives]. We never [thing to avoid]." Claude holds that context for the whole conversation.

2

Stack your requestsClaude handles complex, multi-part prompts well. Ask for a caption, a story script, and a hashtag set in one go — you'll get all three, coherently.

03
The Research Assistant

Gemini

Google · Available at gemini.google.com

Gemini is Google's AI — and it behaves like it. It responds especially well to structured, specific prompts, and it integrates directly into Google Docs and Sheets for a seamless workflow. Use it to build your content research base: topic lists, sourced statistics, or content calendars that live in your Drive.

Example Prompt

"List 5 evidence-based benefits of mindfulness for workplace stress. For each one, write a single LinkedIn-ready sentence I could use in a post. Cite a source for each claim."

What to Expect

Structured, well-organised output — numbered, sourced, formatted. The writing can feel slightly formal compared to ChatGPT or Claude, but the credibility layer is genuinely useful. Run this in Google Docs to edit the output directly without copy-pasting.

1

Specify your format upfrontAsk for "a numbered list", "a table with two columns", or "bullet points with bolded headings." Gemini is a strong formatter — tell it what shape you want.

2

Use it inside Google DocsThe "Help me write" button in Google Docs runs Gemini directly in your document. Great for drafting proposals, content briefs, or email sequences without leaving your workflow.

04
The Fact Checker

Perplexity

Perplexity AI · Available at perplexity.ai

Perplexity is an AI search engine — it browses the web in real time and returns cited sources with every answer. For any business in health, wellness, finance, or another credibility-sensitive field, this is your fact-checking partner before you post. Don't publish a claim you can't back up. Perplexity helps you find the backing.

Example Prompt

"What does recent research say about the link between mindfulness practice and reduced anxiety? Give me 3 specific findings, each with the source and year, that I could reference in a social media post."

What to Expect

Real-time, sourced results — with links to actual studies or reputable publications. This is not marketing copy; it's research infrastructure. Use what you find here to strengthen the claims in the copy you write elsewhere. Always check sources directly before publishing anything health-adjacent.

1

Use it before you write, not afterPerplexity builds your credibility foundation. Gather your supporting evidence first, then take those facts into ChatGPT or Claude to write the actual post.

2

Ask follow-up questionsPerplexity keeps full context across a conversation. Start broad ("what does research say about X?") then narrow: "What about specifically in small business owners?" or "Any Australian studies?"

05
The Visual Copy Partner

Canva AI

Magic Write · Available inside Canva

Canva's Magic Write is built for one thing: getting words onto a design quickly. It's not a deep writing tool, but when you're already in Canva building a post and need a caption or headline, it's fast and surprisingly capable for short-form copy. Keep your prompts short, and it delivers.

Example Prompt

"Short Instagram caption for a mindfulness workshop. Warm and inviting. Maximum 3 sentences."

What to Expect

Brief, clean copy that fits naturally next to a visual design. It won't have much nuance or brand depth, but for a quick headline or two-sentence caption on a designed post, it does the job without breaking your workflow. Expect to edit the output lightly.

1

Shorter prompts, better outputMagic Write is optimised for speed, not depth. Keep prompts under 20 words. Anything longer tends to produce generic results.

2

Design first, then promptUse Magic Write after your visual concept is set — the design context naturally shapes what copy feels right. Starting with the design makes the writing ask clearer.

Where to Start

Your Starter Stack

You don't need to master all five platforms at once. Here's a practical recommendation for most small business owners:

Writing captions, emails & content: Start with ChatGPT for fast drafts, switch to Claude when you need it to sound unmistakably like you.
Research & fact-checking: Use Perplexity before you write anything claim-heavy, especially in health, finance, or coaching.
Planning & Google Workspace: Gemini lives in your Docs already — use it for content briefs and structured planning.
Quick design copy: Canva AI is fine for headlines and short captions when you're already in the design tool.

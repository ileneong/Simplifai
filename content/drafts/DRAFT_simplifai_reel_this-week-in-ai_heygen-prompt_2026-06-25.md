# HeyGen prompt — "This Week in AI" Ep.1 (2026-06-25)

Lane B (AI presenter/avatar) version of the talking-head Reel. Two ways to use it:
**(A)** paste the scene scripts into HeyGen's scene editor (recommended, most control), or
**(B)** paste the one-paragraph prompt into HeyGen's prompt-to-video.

**Output spec:** 9:16 vertical (1080×1920) · 38–42s · captions ON · brand colour background.

---

## A. Scene-by-scene (paste into HeyGen scene editor)

For each scene: **Script** = exact avatar speech · **On-screen text** = overlay you add · **Visual** = background / b-roll.

### Scene 1 — Hook (~4s)
- **Script:** "An AI can now answer your customer DMs and book appointments for you. And that's just one of three things that changed in AI this week. Plain English, two minutes."
- **On-screen text:** Pill "THIS WEEK IN AI" + headline "An AI can now answer your DMs for you."
- **Visual:** Avatar on flat coral (#ff7a59) background, chest-up framing, high energy.

### Scene 2 — Canva AI 2.0 (~10s)
- **Script:** "First, Canva launched AI two point oh. You describe what you want in plain words, like a six slide carousel about your new service, and it builds a near finished, on brand draft. Everything stays editable, and it remembers your brand. That blank canvas freeze that stops you? Gone."
- **On-screen text:** "1. Canva builds the design while you type"
- **Visual:** Cut to screen-recording of a Canva prompt generating a design; avatar voice continues over it (or avatar in a corner inset).

### Scene 3 — ChatGPT memory (~10s)
- **Script:** "Second, ChatGPT rebuilt its memory this month. Roughly double what it held before, plus controls to see and edit what it knows about you. So it can finally hold your brand voice and your customer across chats. You stop re explaining your business every time."
- **On-screen text:** "2. Stop re-explaining your business to ChatGPT"
- **Visual:** Avatar, then a quick screen-grab of ChatGPT memory settings. Blur anything personal.

### Scene 4 — Meta DM agent (~10s)
- **Script:** "And third, the one I opened with. Meta's AI agent can now answer customer questions and book appointments right inside your Instagram DMs. Genuinely useful, with one honest catch. Let it be the first responder, not the voice of your brand. Set guardrails and hand off to a human, or it costs you the trust you're building."
- **On-screen text:** "3. AI can now answer your Instagram DMs"
- **Visual:** Avatar to camera for the caveat (the human nuance is the point), brief b-roll of a phone with DMs.

### Scene 5 — CTA / loop (~6s)
- **Script:** "None of these is a magic trick. They're pieces you plug into a system you run in a few hours a week, with you still in control. Save this, try one, and follow for next week's three."
- **On-screen text:** "Save this. Try one this week. Follow for next week."
- **Visual:** Avatar + "pin·go" wordmark lower-third. End on the coral hook frame so a replay loops cleanly.

## B. One-paragraph prompt (paste into HeyGen prompt-to-video)

> Create a 40 second vertical 9:16 social video for "Simplifai," an AI company that helps small business owners run their own marketing. A warm, friendly female presenter speaks straight to camera in a conversational, upbeat tone (not hyped), against a flat coral background, with bold captions on. She says: "An AI can now answer your customer DMs and book appointments for you, and that's just one of three things that changed in AI this week. First, Canva launched AI 2.0 — describe what you want in plain words and it builds a near finished, on brand design you can still edit. Second, ChatGPT rebuilt its memory with roughly double the capacity, so it holds your brand voice and your customer across chats and you stop re explaining your business. Third, the one I opened with: Meta's AI agent can now answer questions and book appointments inside your Instagram DMs, but let it be the first responder, not the voice of your brand, and always hand off to a human. None of these is a magic trick. They're pieces of a system you run in a few hours a week, with you still in control. Save this, try one, and follow for next week's three." Use snappy cuts, large bold yellow and white captions, and end on a coral title card reading "This week in AI."

## HeyGen settings (set these in the project)

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| **Aspect ratio** | 9:16 portrait |
| **Avatar** | Warm, approachable, business-casual. Use your own custom/Instant Avatar if you have one (builds founder familiarity over the series); otherwise a friendly studio avatar. |
| **Voice** | Conversational and warm, energetic but unhurried, medium pace, friendly. Avoid the flat "news anchor" read. If using ElevenLabs voice in HeyGen, pick a natural conversational profile. |
| **Background** | Flat coral `#ff7a59` (Scheme B). Use green-screen/remove-background so the avatar sits on the brand colour. |
| **Captions** | ON. Sentence case. Keep them above the bottom safe zone (~420px). Brand-style if HeyGen allows custom fonts. |
| **Safe zones** | Top 220px, bottom 420px, right 180px kept clear of avatar face and text. |
| **Music** | Add a low, upbeat track under the voice on export, or swap in trending audio when you upload to Instagram. |

## Pronunciation / TTS notes
- "AI 2.0" → written as "two point oh" in the script so TTS reads it cleanly.
- "DMs" reads fine as is; if HeyGen spells it out oddly, write "D.M.s".
- "re explaining" kept as two words on purpose so TTS doesn't trip on the hyphen.
- No em dashes anywhere (house style + cleaner TTS phrasing).

- [x] Claims traceable: Canva newsroom, OpenAI release notes (early June 2026), Meta via TechCrunch (June 2026). Avatar names the companies; full sources stay in the caption.

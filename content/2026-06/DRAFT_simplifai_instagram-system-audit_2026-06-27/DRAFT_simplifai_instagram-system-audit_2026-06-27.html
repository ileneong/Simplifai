Six copies of the same tool. Nobody noticed.
What we found when we audited a client's setup, and why it matters for yours.
01 / 06
First, what is an audit
A spring clean for the tools you work in.
Systems get messy the same way a junk drawer does. Quietly, a little at a time. An audit is someone stopping to look at the whole thing at once.
02 / 06
Finding one
Every copy had drifted a little.
Six near identical versions of one tool, each tweaked over time. We replaced all of them with a single one that does the job for everyone.
03 / 06
Finding two
Two guides quietly disagreed.
Each one kept its own copy of the same instructions, and over months they grew apart. Now both point to one source, so they can never split again.
04 / 06
Finding three
Clutter nobody chose to keep.
Old files no one used, and junk getting saved on every change. We cleared it out and set a simple rule so it stops piling back up.
05 / 06
One source of truth beats six drifting copies.
That is what an audit buys you. Less to maintain, fewer mistakes, a system you can actually trust. Save this for your next clean up.

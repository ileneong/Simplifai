Most carousels don't flop because of bad taste. They flop because there's no system behind them. Once you have the system, you can build one in a few hours, on brand, every time.

Here's the whole method, and it's the same one we use for the brands we work with.

1. Give every slide one job. A carousel is an argument, not a photo album. Hook, then the problem, then two or three ideas, then a clear ask. One idea per slide, and each slide has to make sense on its own.

2. Lock three colours and two fonts. One bold font for headlines, one clean font for the rest. Three colours, used the same way every time. Same logo, same corner, every slide. That repetition is the thing people start to recognise as you.

3. Win the thumbnail. Slide one is the only slide most people ever see. One idea, huge type, readable at thumbnail size. If it doesn't stop the scroll, nothing behind it gets read.

That's it. Not talent. A repeatable structure you fill in each week.

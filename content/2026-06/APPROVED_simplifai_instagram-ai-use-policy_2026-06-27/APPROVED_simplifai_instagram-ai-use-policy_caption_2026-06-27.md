Most small business owners will never write an AI use policy. It is the one thing that quietly builds trust, and almost nobody does it.

Here is the good news: it fits on one page. About ten honest lines.

We pulled this from the free AI Fluency for Small Business course (a PayPal and Anthropic collaboration), and shaped it into a checklist you can actually use:

1. What you use AI for. Name the real jobs, like drafting captions or summarising notes.
2. What never goes in. The data that stays out of every model, like customer details and payment info.
3. Who checks it. The person who reviews AI output before it ships.
4. How you tell people. The way you disclose AI involvement to your customers.

That is it. Ten lines that turn a quiet worry into a visible sign of credibility.

Save this post, open a doc, and draft yours this week. Your future self (and your customers) will thank you.

# Caption — Talk, do not type (ChatGPT dictation)

**Post:** 6-slide carousel · Scheme D (Light/Neutral) · What's New in AI
**Date:** 2026-06-30

---

The hardest part of staying visible online was never the ideas. It was finding a quiet hour at a keyboard to write them down.

So here is the update worth knowing this week: ChatGPT just improved its dictation, and it is on every plan, free included. You tap the mic, talk, and it writes it up cleanly. Languages and accents are handled far better than before, even if you switch mid-sentence.

For a busy owner, that changes where the work happens. Between clients, in the car park, on a walk, you can talk out a caption or a client follow-up and have a real first draft waiting.

How to try it:
1. Open ChatGPT and tap the mic.
2. Talk through one caption the way you would explain it to a friend.
3. Tighten the draft afterwards. That part stays yours.

Two honest notes. It writes the way you speak, so the first pass will ramble, you are still the editor. And keep client details out of it. Never say someone's name or health information out loud into a tool, talk in general terms and fill the specifics in yourself.

Source: OpenAI, ChatGPT release notes, June 2026.

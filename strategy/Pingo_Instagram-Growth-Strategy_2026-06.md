## 1. Executive summary

Pingo grows on Instagram by becoming the **most credible and genuinely useful AI-for-small-business voice in the feed — not the loudest.** Our audience are experts at what they do and tired of being invisible online. They don't follow hype; they follow the account that quietly makes them feel capable.

So our follower engine is simple: **publish content so useful people save it and send it to a friend — then give them a clear reason to follow for the rest of the system.** Reels earn the reach. Carousels earn the authority and the save. Honest, disclosure-led posts earn the trust that converts a viewer into a follower.

The single biggest lever right now is not a new tactic — it's **showing up again.** We've been dark for 37 days. Restarting a consistent rhythm, with follow-specific CTAs we never used in Week 1, is the whole game for the next 90 days.

## 3. Growth goals & KPIs

Follower growth is the headline number, but it's a *lagging* metric. We grow followers by moving the *leading* ones:

| Metric | Why it matters | Target trend (90 days) |
|---|---|---|
| **Follower count** | Headline goal | Steady week-over-week climb; no flat weeks |
| **Reach / accounts reached** | New people = new follow chances | Up, driven mainly by Reels |
| **Saves** | Strongest signal of "useful" → authority | Up on carousels (system-teaching) |
| **Shares / sends** | Borrowed reach + warm new audiences | Up on reframe + proof posts |
| **Profile visits** | The pre-follow step | Track visit → follow rate |
| **Follow-conversion rate** | Visits that become follows | Improve as bio/Highlights/pins are optimised |

**Directional 90-day goal:** establish a reliable cadence and a positive, compounding follower curve — prioritise *consistency and follow-rate* over any single viral spike.

**Weekly review (lightweight, every Monday ~30 min):** pull IG insights, note top-3 posts by saves and by shares, log what theme/format/hook they used, and pick one thing to do more of next week. Keep a running one-line log per week so patterns surface over a month.

## 4. Audience & positioning

**Who:** Small-business owners — solo practitioners, 2–15 staff teams, independent founders. They're genuine experts in their field with **no marketing background**, limited time, and a quiet frustration: *they're good at what they do, and almost nobody online knows it.*

**The tension that earns the follow:** They've been told they need to "post more," "go viral," "use AI." It feels overwhelming, inauthentic, and off-brand. Pingo's promise is the opposite of hustle: *a system you run in a few hours a week that keeps you in control and keeps your brand consistent.*

**Why our voice converts:** *Trustworthy before trendy. Authoritative before aesthetic.* In a feed full of AI hype and "10x your reach" noise, the calm, credible, evidence-informed voice is the one a serious business owner trusts enough to follow. We don't sell magic. We hand over a system.

**Tagline anchor:** *"You bring the expertise. We bring the system. Your AI marketing team — built in a weekend."*

## 5. Content pillars — the reason to follow

Four repeatable pillars, each tied to Pingo's voice rule (every post must **educate, motivate, or build community**) and each running as a recognisable *series* so a new follower knows exactly what they're signing up for.

| Pillar | Primary format | Job | Drives | Example angles |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **① System teaching** | Carousel | Educate | **Saves** | "The 4 pieces of an AI marketing system," "How to write a brief AI can actually use," "Your brand-voice doc in 5 fields" |
| **② Myth / reframe** | Reel + single post | Motivate | **Shares** | "Most people use AI like a vending machine," "Two camps on AI — both wrong," "Invisible isn't a talent problem, it's a system problem" |
| **③ Honest limits / credibility** | Single post + Reel | Build trust | **Follows** | "5 things AI genuinely can't do for your marketing," "We disclose AI — here's why," "Where AI is dangerous for small businesses" |
| **④ Proof in action** | Reel + carousel | Motivate | **Aspiration → follow** | "A full week of content, done by Tuesday," "What a system looks like vs. winging it," behind-the-scenes of the Stack |

**Series naming idea:** brand the recurring formats so they're followable — e.g. *"The System Files"* (pillar ①), *"Reframe of the week"* (pillar ②), *"What AI can't do"* (pillar ③). A named series is a subscription people choose to follow.

## 6. Format & cadence plan

**Weekly rhythm (sustainable target):**
- **3–5 Reels/week** — primary *reach / new-follower* engine.
- **3–4 feed posts/week** (carousels + single posts) — primary *save / authority* engine.
- **Daily Stories** — low-effort presence: polls, "ask me anything," reshare top posts, behind-the-scenes. Stories keep existing followers warm and feed Highlights.

**Format roles:**
- **Reels** = top of funnel. Text-led or faceless, trending audio, fast hook, one idea. Built to be *shared*.
- **Carousels** = mid funnel. Teach a system step-by-step. Built to be *saved*. Slide 1 = thumb-stop headline; last slide = follow CTA.
- **Single posts** = punchy reframes and credibility statements. Built to be *sent*.

**Design continuity (from the Pingo IG design system):**
- Stay on the **4:5 / 1080×1350** canvas. Never square.
- Continue the **4-scheme rotation, never two consecutive posts on the same scheme.** Last scheme used was **C (Purple Hero)** on Day 7 — **the next post is due on Scheme A (Yellow Hero).**
- **One accent element per post** (asterisk, circle underline, bracket, dot cluster, pull-quote mark) — keep visual variety without clutter.
- Headline readable at thumbnail size; max ~8–10 words per line; sentence case.

## 7. Follower-growth tactics (the core)

### a) Hook discipline
The first line / first frame decides reach. Tune every hook to the Pingo voice (calm, credible, specific — never clickbait).
- **Formulas that fit us:** the honest admission ("You're not behind — you're just unsystemised."), the reframe ("Most people use AI like a vending machine."), the specific payoff ("A full week of content, done by Tuesday."), the gentle call-out ("If you're brilliant at your job and invisible online, this is for you.").
- **Avoid:** fearmongering, "secret hack," fake urgency. AI-cringe kills credibility, and credibility is our whole moat.

### b) Follow-specific CTAs (the Week-1 miss, fixed)
Every post gets a reason to follow, rotated so it never feels needy:
- **Series cliffhanger:** "This is part 1 of 4. Follow so part 2 lands in your feed tomorrow."
- **Identity hook:** "Follow if you're an expert who's tired of being invisible online."
- **Value promise:** "We post one usable AI marketing system every week. Follow along."
- Keep the proven **"comment *prompt*" → DM the prompt template** as a secondary engagement CTA, but pair it with a follow ask.

### c) Engineering saves & shares
- **Saves:** checklist carousels, swipe-file prompts, "save this for your next content day." Anything reference-able.
- **Shares:** "Send this to a business owner drowning in their own marketing." Relatable reframes and one-liners are the most-sent format.

### d) Reels for reach
- Produce the outlined **8-Reel June batch**: faceless / text-led, trending audio, ≤30s, one idea each. Reuse the proven Week-1 themes as Reel scripts.
- Run the **Lane B AI-avatar pilot** on one Reel and compare reach + saves vs. the faceless lane. Let data — not preference — decide whether avatars stay.
- Reels are where *new* people find us, so weight pillars ② (reframe) and ④ (proof) here.

### e) Profile as a conversion page (no website — the profile IS the funnel)
- **Bio:** crisp value prop + identity + soft CTA, no URL. Lead with "AI marketing team for small business owners."
- **Name field (the searchable one):** include keywords like "AI Marketing for Small Business" — this is IG SEO and drives discovery.
- **Highlights as proof:** "How it works," "The system," "What AI can't do," "Wins" — turn the best content into an always-on tour.
- **Pinned trio:** pin our single best reframe (reach), best system carousel (authority), and best proof post (aspiration) so every profile visitor hits our strongest three.

### f) Engagement loops
- **First-60-minutes window:** reply to every comment in the first hour of a post — it compounds reach and signals a real community.
- **DM automation** for the prompt magnet (comment → auto-DM the template), then a human follow-up. Value first, soft follow ask second.
- **Outbound community engagement:** spend 15 min/day leaving genuine, useful comments on adjacent small-business / founder accounts. Borrow their audience by being visibly helpful, not promotional.

### g) Distribution & collaborations
- **IG Collab posts** and shoutout swaps with adjacent (non-competing) small-business educators, bookkeepers, web designers, coaches — shared audiences, one post in two feeds.
- Repurpose top carousels into Reels and vice versa to multiply reach from one idea.

### h) Hashtag / SEO
- Keep captions keyword-rich (IG ranks caption text now).
- Small, focused tag set built on the proven base — **#SmallBusiness #AIMarketing #MarketingTips** — plus 5–8 rotating niche tags (e.g. #SmallBusinessOwner, #SoloBusiness, #ContentStrategy, #MarketingForBeginners). Avoid giant generic tags that bury us.

## 9. Measurement & iteration

- **Weekly (Mondays):** top-3 by saves, top-3 by shares, follower delta, profile-visit → follow rate. One sentence: "do more of ___ next week."
- **Double down** on the pillar/format/hook that consistently drives saves and shares.
- **Kill criteria:** if a format underperforms on reach *and* saves for 3 posts running, retire or rework it.
- **Avatar-pilot gate (end of week 4):** keep Lane B only if it matches or beats the faceless lane on reach and saves.

---

## 10. Compliance & brand guardrails

- **No website / no URLs, ever.** The profile and DMs are the funnel.
- **Disclose AI assistance** in captions — transparency is part of our credibility.
- **No fabricated results or testimonials**; only use client outcomes with explicit consent.
- **No fearmongering, no unsubstantiated claims, no AI-cringe.** Authority-first, always.
- For any future content touching regulated topics (health, finance, legal), flag for review and avoid absolute claims.
